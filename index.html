The Paradox of Moving Charges

üîë Key Background

Maxwell's theory of electromagnetism predicts that the speed of light is a universal constant - the same for all observers, regardless of their motion.


This directly contradicts Galilean relativity, which says velocities should add and subtract when changing reference frames.


This incompatibility suggests something deeper: Either Maxwell's electromagnetism or Galilean relativity (or both!) must be modified. Einstein chose to keep Maxwell's theory and modify our understanding of space and time!

üéØ Resolving the Paradox

The Solution: Electric Fields Transform!

The resolution comes from special relativity:


Key Insight: Electric and magnetic fields are not independent - they are two aspects of the same electromagnetic field.

When you change reference frames:
  • What appears as a magnetic field in one frame...
  • ...may appear as an electric field (or mix of both) in another frame!
What Actually Happens

Lab frame (original):

  • Wire is electrically neutral (equal positive and negative charges)
  • No electric field
  • Magnetic field exists (due to current)
  • Magnetic force on moving positive charge

Moving frame:

  • Due to length contraction (special relativity!), the spacing between charges changes
  • The wire appears to have a net charge density
  • This creates an electric field
  • Electric force on the (now stationary) positive charge
  • No magnetic field (no current)
The Deep Connection
This reveals something profound:

‚Ä¢ Galilean relativity (simple velocity addition) is incompatible with electromagnetism

‚Ä¢ Maxwell's equations predicted the constancy of the speed of light

‚Ä¢ To make electromagnetism work in all reference frames, Einstein had to develop special relativity

‚Ä¢ In special relativity, space and time mix together, and so do electric and magnetic fields!
Bottom Line: The same force exists in both frames, but it manifests as a magnetic force in one frame and an electric force in another. Both observers measure the same physical effect (attraction toward the wire), but they explain it using different components of the electromagnetic field.

This is only consistent if we use special relativity, where the speed of light is constant and the Lorentz transformations (not Galilean transformations) connect different reference frames.

üéì Key Takeaways

  • Electromagnetic fields are relative: What you call "electric" vs "magnetic" depends on your reference frame
  • Maxwell + Galileo = Contradiction: Classical velocity addition breaks electromagnetism
  • Special relativity saves the day: New transformation laws preserve Maxwell's equations
  • Length contraction is real: It creates the electric field in the moving frame
  • Physics is invariant: All observers see the same force, just described differently
